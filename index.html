General of the Army Raden Sudirman

The man who defend the nation with rebel heart

Here's a time line of Raden Sudirman:

  • 1916 - Born in Purbalingga, Dutch East Indies
  • 1936 - Began working as a teacher, and later headmaster, at a Muhammadiyah-run elementary school
  • 1944 - Joined the Japanese-sponsored Defenders of the Homeland as a battalion commander in Banyumas
  • 1945 - Met with President Soekarno and asked for defending nation
  • 1948 - Faced with internal dissent and tuberculosis
  • 1949 - Retired at Magelang
  • 1950 - Died at age 34, slightly more than a month after the Dutch recognised Indonesia's independence.
"Sudirman is impossible to control and hard-headed, but ultimately intent on doing what was right for the country; although Sudirman often did not like the government's position, he would generally obey his orders."

-- Indonesia first Vice President Moh. Hatta
